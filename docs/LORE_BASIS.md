# Lore Basis

> This document exists to guide designers, enemy creators, and level builders. It is not a narrative bible. It is a design constraint document dressed as lore.

## World Premise

The world underwent the **Flattening** — an ontological catastrophe that reduced complex beings into simplified geometric forms. This was not an explosion or a plague. It was a *compression*. Meaning itself collapsed. People, animals, structures, and ideas lost dimensionality until they became the simplest shape that could still hold their function.

The world is the aftermath. Cities exist as skeletal frameworks. Crowds exist as clusters of identical triangles. A hospital is a grid of cubes that pulse faintly. A school is an empty lattice that hums.

Nobody remembers what happened. The shapes don't remember being people.

## What "Becoming a Shape" Means

- **Not transformation** — reduction. A person doesn't *turn into* a triangle. Their complexity collapses until only the triangle remains.
- **Shape = role**: a guard becomes a rigid rectangle. A teacher becomes an open lattice. A child becomes a spinning sphere.
- **Behavior = identity**: shapes don't think. They perform the last function they can hold. A triangle that was once a soldier attacks. A cube that was once a builder stacks.
- **No reversal**: shapes cannot become people again. The information is lost.

## What the Player Is

The player is an **unstable form** — a shape that hasn't finished collapsing. They flicker between states. They can still adapt, choose, and react in ways that fixed shapes cannot.

The player doesn't know *why* they're unstable. They might be:
- The last person still collapsing (slowest reduction)
- A glitch in the Flattening (error state)
- Something that was already abstract before the collapse (a concept that gained form)

**Design implication**: the player's visual form should subtly shift — slight vertex noise, color instability, occasional geometry flicker. They are not solid.

## What Enemies Are

Enemies are **collapsed humans performing residual functions**. They are not evil. They are not conscious. They are compulsions given form.

- A **Triangle Shard** was a person who defended something. Now it lunges.
- A **Cube Sentinel** was a person who watched. Now it fires.
- A **Spiral Dancer** was a person who evaded. Now it darts.

Enemies attack the player because the player is *unstable* — an unresolved form in a world that demands resolution. The player is noise. Shapes seek signal.

## Why Environments Exist

Environments are **collapsed places**. They retain the spatial logic of their former purpose but lose specificity.

- A library becomes a hall of floating rectangular slabs
- A train station becomes parallel lines converging at a vanishing point
- A home becomes a small box with a single warm light that flickers

Environments are not random. They are the ghosts of function.

## What the Conflict Is

The player moves toward the **Convergence Point** — the center of the Flattening. Something there is either:
- The cause (and can be stopped)
- The completion (and the player must choose whether to finish the process or resist)
- Nothing (and the journey was the point)

**Design implication**: the game does not need to resolve this. The conflict is the forward push. Bosses are gatekeepers — shapes so complex they absorbed many people's functions. They are amalgamations. They guard transitions between zones.

## Symbolic Motifs

| Motif | Meaning | Usage |
|-------|---------|-------|
| **Edges** | Aggression, sharpness, loss of softness | Enemy attack shapes, damage zones |
| **Hollowness** | Absence, the space where identity was | Room centers, boss arenas, player death effect |
| **Repetition** | Loss of individuality, pattern replacing person | Enemy crowds, environment tiling, ambient sound |
| **Flickering** | Instability, residual humanity, the player | Player visual, interactable objects, lore echoes |
| **Convergence** | Everything pulling inward, compression | Zone progression, boss designs, environmental geometry |

## Boss Themes

### Boss 1: The Aggregate
- **Shape**: a cluster of many small triangles fused into a shifting humanoid silhouette
- **Concept**: a crowd that became one — many people compressed into a single form
- **Mechanic implication**: splits into smaller enemies, reforms, attacks change based on current mass

### Boss 2: The Frame
- **Shape**: a wireframe outline of a house/building that walks and collapses
- **Concept**: a place that became a creature — a structure that still tries to shelter but can only crush
- **Mechanic implication**: creates enclosed zones, walls that close in, attacks by reshaping its interior space

### Boss 3: The Mirror
- **Shape**: a flat reflective polygon that copies the player's silhouette
- **Concept**: a shape that defines itself by reflecting others — identity as pure mimicry
- **Mechanic implication**: mirrors player attacks with delay, forces the player to fight their own patterns
